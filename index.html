The diagnosis, in one paragraph

Your reported misses — slice/push off the tee and thin contact with woods and long irons — are the textbook signature of early extension plus an open clubface. The pelvis thrusts toward the ball in transition, you stand up to make room, your spine tilts away from the target, and the face stays open. The result is what you described: woods off the tee, duffed long irons.

GOLFTEC's SwingTRU study of more than 90 million swings identified six body positions that correlate most strongly with handicap. Two map directly onto your pattern: pros average 36° of hip turn at impact vs. 19.5° for high-handicaps, and 32° of forward shoulder bend at the finish vs. just 3.2°. That second number is the single biggest swing-shape gap in the entire dataset, and it’s a near-perfect description of standing up through impact.

Where the data comes from

  • GOLFTEC SwingTRU Motion Study — 90M+ swings, 30K-golfer database, 48 body positions analyzed.

  • HackMotion — over 1M tracked swings on lead-wrist mechanics at impact.
  • Trackman & Arccos — launch monitor benchmarks and 4M+ rounds of strokes-gained data.
  • Adam Young — The Practice Manual for practice-design framework.
  • Vision54 (Pia Nilsson, Lynn Marriott) — the Think Box / Play Box pre-shot routine.
  • Motor-learning meta-analyses in Scientific Reports (2024) and Frontiers (2024) on contextual interference.
