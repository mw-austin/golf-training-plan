Phase 1 · Posture & Setup
10 minutes a day, 15 minutes a week

Mobility & strength

The four physical limitations TPI ties to early extension and loss of posture — tight hip flexors, limited hip internal rotation, restricted thoracic rotation, limited ankle dorsiflexion — are all addressed below. Plus a 15-minute weekly strength micro-routine.

10-minute mobility routine

Do this before every session. Not optional — it’s a direct fix for the cause of your miss pattern.

ExerciseDoseHow to / why
90/90 hip stretch60 sec/sideSit on the floor, both knees bent at 90°, one in front (lead), one to the side (trail). Lean forward over the lead knee. Improves hip internal rotation, the #1 limit on rotation.
Half-kneeling hip flexor stretch45 sec/sideLunge position, back knee on ground. Squeeze the back glute, push hips forward. Tight hip flexors are a direct contributor to early extension.
Quadruped T-spine rotation8 reps/sideOn hands and knees, hand behind head. Rotate elbow up to ceiling, then down under the lead arm. Restricted thoracic rotation forces the over-the-top move.
Wall ankle dorsiflexion10 reps/sideToes 3–4 inches from wall, drive front knee to touch wall without lifting heel. Limited ankle dorsiflexion forces the ‘standing up’ compensation.
Glute bridge12 repsOn your back, knees bent. Drive heels into the ground, lift hips. Squeeze glutes at the top, hold 2 sec.
Side-lying clamshell12 reps/sideOn your side, knees bent and stacked, feet together. Open top knee like a clamshell. Activates glute medius — direct stabilizer of the pelvis during rotation.
If you only have 5 minutes, drop the clamshell and ankle work and keep everything else.

Optional 15-minute weekly strength micro-routine

Optional but high-leverage. The TPI screens that most predict early extension are glute strength, core/anti-rotation strength, and hip stability. Three 15-minute sessions a week — living room, no equipment beyond a single dumbbell or kettlebell — covers the highest-impact movements. If you only have one session a week, do the Big Three.

The Big Three (every session)

ExerciseDoseWhy
Goblet squat (or bodyweight squat)3 × 10Loaded hip flexion and extension. Builds the squat pattern that early extension is a compensation for. Hold a single dumbbell at your chest.
Romanian deadlift (single dumbbell)3 × 10Hip hinge pattern — same shape as your golf posture. Builds glute and hamstring strength that supports a stable pelvis.
Pallof press (with band) or anti-rotation plank3 × 10/sideAnti-rotation core work. The core’s job in the golf swing is to resist rotation, not create it.

Bonus (rotate in if you have time)

  • Side plank with leg lift — 3 × 8/side. Hits glute medius under more load than the clamshell.
  • Reverse lunge with rotation — 3 × 6/side. Single-leg balance plus thoracic rotation.
  • Med-ball rotational throw (into a wall) — 3 × 5/side. Power production through rotation.

Frequency: 1–3 sessions per week, 15 min each. Best slotted on Tue/Thu/Sat (off-range days). Do not do this on the same day as a long range session.
