Phase 1 · Posture & Setup
What to buy, what to skip, when to fit

Equipment & aids

You don’t need much. The whole plan can be run with what you already own plus three small purchases.

Get these

  • Two extra alignment sticks (~$15). Used for the path gate, foot-line setup checks, and a target-line reference on every range session.
  • Impact spray or impact tape (~$10–$25). Use once a week on Day 2 to verify your strike pattern matches the launch monitor smash data.
  • Small mirror for indoor work (~$25, optional). 30 seconds of address-position checking each morning costs nothing and reinforces the new posture.

Skip these

  • Personal launch monitor (Mevo+, Garmin R10, etc.). Nice to have, but you have bay access. Save the money.
  • Swing-plane training aid (Tour Striker Plane Mate, etc.). The wall and chair drills do the same job for free.
  • New driver. Equipment is downstream of swing pattern. Reassess in week 9, not now.

If you’re considering a club fitting

Wait. A fitting before week 8 will fit you to your current pattern, including the slice. After week 8, when face-to-path is closer to neutral, a fitting will produce numbers and a head/shaft combination that actually serve you. If you fit now, you may end up with a club that compensates for the slice — and once the slice is gone, the club fights you.
