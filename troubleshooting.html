When things break (and they will)

When things break

Every plan looks great on paper. Then week 3 happens, the slice comes back, and you start adding swing thoughts to fix it. Don’t. Use this instead.

If the slice comes back mid-phase

  1. Check your grip in the mirror. Most slice returns are grip drift, not swing breakdown.
  2. Spend the next session 100% on Wall Drill + Lead-Wrist Flexion. No driver.
  3. Re-baseline on the monitor next session. If face-to-path is back above +5°, repeat the previous phase for 3 sessions before progressing.

If you start fatting / chunking

  1. This is almost always the wall drill correction overdone — you’re getting stuck in the trail leg.
  2. Add the Step-Through Drill to the next two sessions.
  3. Move ball position back 1 ball-width with irons until the strike returns to center.

If you have an awful round

Don’t go to the range the next day. Take a rest day or do the Bonus Day routine. The worst response to a bad round is to grind through 100 balls trying to find ‘it.’ That’s how new bad habits get built.

If you’re not seeing progress at week 4

  1. Pull the launch monitor data. Are the KPIs moving in the right direction even if the score isn’t yet? Score lags pattern by weeks.
  2. Book a single 30-minute lesson with a teaching pro. Bring this site and the launch monitor data. Ask: “Is my pattern moving the way it should be? What single feel would you add?” Don’t get a swing rebuild — get a course correction.
  3. Re-read the diagnosis. If your real miss is something other than slice/thin, the drill prescription changes.
